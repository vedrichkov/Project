Phase 2 cohorts, after baseline

Five additional cohorts not in phase 1. Once the trio establishes a CAC baseline, the strongest reserve gets promoted in phase 2. These are untapped audiences, not panic insurance. Each one is outcome-driven, most feature a named Polish teacher on camera.

Reserve 1
Time-poor, exhausted
The recovered evening
Time-saved reveal
Open on a kitchen table at 23:00, papers everywhere, parent and kid hunched over homework. Time-lapse spans 30 days. Cut to the same kitchen at 18:30, the kid working alone on a tablet, the parent on the sofa with a book. Single line on screen: “Day 1, 23:00. Day 30, 18:30.” The recovered evening is the hero.
Reserve 2
Guilt-aware, financially-stretched
She knew it was expensive too
Unspoken guilt reveal
Open on a bank statement, the recurring tutoring expense highlighted. Cut to the kid at the doorway, noticing her parent's tired face, looking away. Mother's voiceover lands as the camera holds on the kid: “She felt the tutoring bill. Now she doesn't have to.” Close on the same kitchen, kid relaxed at the tablet, parent unstressed in the background.
Reserve 3
Detail-conscious, subject-specific
No more crossed-out words
Subject-specific outcome
Open on a Polish notebook page, full of red corrections, the kid's frustration visible in the handwriting. Cut to a named Polish teacher on camera (name + school + photo), walking through one ortografia rule in 20 seconds. Close on a fresh notebook page, clean writing, the kid smiling. End frame: “The page is finally clean.”
Reserve 4
Regional, tutor-scarce
Opole, tonight
Geo-cut deployment overlay
Five regional cuts of the same 20-second ad, identical beats and pacing, only the opening line changes per voivodeship. “In Opole, a private tutor is a luxury. Eduboom, tonight.” Same shape for Lublin, Łódź, Wrocław, Katowice. Each version times its flight to that region's mid-term test calendar so the message lands in-window, not blended into national noise.
Reserve 5
Trust-driven, credentials-respecting
The named-teacher testimonial
Outcome + trust signal
Open on a parent at the kitchen table, holding two report cards, real, not posed. They reveal the first card: a 3 in red. Cut to weeks later, the parent reveals the second card: a 5 in green. Cut to a named Polish teacher on camera (name + school + photo) closing with one line: “From a 3 to a 5, in one term.” Clean white space, no music until the reveal.
