The business case

Spend, timing, and decision rules behind the launch.

The bet at a glance

Find the cheapest, highest-converting path from a cold Polish parent to their child's first Mission Started, then turn that path into a repeatable engine inside the Sept-Oct demand peak. Both months sit above the PEAK threshold in the calendar below.

Goal Find the cheapest path from a cold Polish parent to a child's first answer-tap inside a real lesson. That tap is what we count as activation. Signup, paywall, and paid conversion all follow from it.
Method
  • 3 ads, one per cohort:
    • AD1, grade jump, skeptical parent
    • AD2, recognition meme, emotional parent
    • AD3, tutoring cost reframe, practical parent
  • 2 channels: Meta + TikTok for awareness, Google Ads for intent.
  • 6 cells total (3 ads × 2 channels), live in parallel from July 1.
When
  • June: production and pre-launch prep.
  • July to August: July 1 full launch, both Paid awareness and Intent capture go live. Phase 1 calibrates all 6 cells at low demand.
  • September to October: Sep 1 transition to Phase 2, the remaining 60% concentrates on winning cells. Phase 2 scales through the back-to-school peak.

The annual demand rhythm

Aggregated from 5 years of weekly PL Trends data. Math is the base bar. Toggle a subject to compare rhythms. Launching July 1 means buying into the trough at calibration spend, deliberately, to read relative cell performance before the Sept peak.

Eight events. Two team targets.

The eight tracked events trace the funnel from first ad click to paid customer. Product owns Mission Started. Marketing and Product share Paid Conversion. Without these firing correctly, nothing is measurable.

◆ Product target Owned by product
mission_started
The moment a child taps the first answer inside a real lesson. This is the activation we count. Each flow decides when to ask for an account, before play, after the first win, or never at all.
◆ Revenue target Shared, marketing + product
paid_conversion
The moment a parent's payment clears (BLIK or card). Marketing brings the parent in. Product converts them. Both teams write the paywall copy together. The CAC ceilings above are measured against this event.

Any recognisable IP used in creative mockups (Disney, Pixar, Universal, music labels) is a reference asset only. Shipping it in paid creative would expose Eduboom to takedown notices, Meta + TikTok account-level strikes (which can pull the whole campaign mid-flight), and reputational risk. Mitigation: every meme is re-illustrated in the Eduboom visual register before launch. Same comedic mechanic, original character, original art, original facial beat. Lock the redrawn assets by June 28, ready for the July 1 launch. No exceptions, no “fair use” arguments.
