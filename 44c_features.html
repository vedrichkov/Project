The backlog

Four features queued after the launch.

Progress report & parent-sent quick test

A live parent dashboard plus the ability to send the child a 4-question verification test on the latest topic, with results delivered straight back to the parent.

Why this matters

A daily parent-side touchpoint between Day 3 and Day 14 closes the gap that's currently empty. Parents who engage daily renew at 2-3× the rate of those who don't, and a parent dashboard is something the private tutor can't offer.

Quiz challenge, beat your classmates

After completing a mission, the kid can challenge their classmates to beat their score on the same questions. Friends learn and compete simultaneously.

After any mission, "Challenge a friend" button. The kid shares a deep link via WhatsApp, Messenger, SMS, or in-app contacts. Friend opens it, sees the score to beat, takes the same quiz. Both see the comparison instantly. Optional weekly leaderboard scoped to the class.

Why this is the highest-leverage virality move

Taps the existing social graph (real classmates) instead of trying to build a new one. Solves the cold-start problem too: the friend doesn't need to sign up to take the challenge, only to challenge back. Uniquely Eduboom-shaped, Squla doesn't have it, Brainly's social model is fundamentally different.

Subtitles & Ukrainian language layer

Ukrainian-language subtitles on every video lesson plus a Ukrainian UI option. Same Polish curriculum content, accessible to Ukrainian-speaking households in Poland.

~1.5M Ukrainian residents in Poland (2026), of whom an estimated 180-220K children sit in Polish schools but live in Ukrainian-speaking households. No major PL edtech competitor addresses this segment. Brainly, Gryzie, Matemaks, Pi-stacja, Squla, all Polish-only.

Why this is strategically distinctive

Every competitor has the same content as Eduboom. Almost none has the same audience flexibility. "The only Polish edtech you can actually use if your home language isn't Polish" is clean positioning that earns press in Ukrainian-Polish community media. Hard to copy because the cost is execution, not a clever idea.

E8 exam prep tests & klasa 8 readiness

Full-length, timed E8 (egzamin ósmoklasisty) practice tests in math, Polish, and English, with diagnostic scoring and a per-topic remediation plan. The single highest-intent annual moment in the Polish parent calendar, wired straight into a paywall.

Every May, ~300,000 klasa-8 students sit the E8 state exit exam. Results gate liceum and technikum admission, turning klasa-8 parents into willing buyers Feb-April. Search volume on "egzamin osmoklasisty matematyka" peaks 3.5-4× baseline in March, again in May. The single highest-intent paid window in the PL calendar.

Without an E8 product, klasa-8 parents bounce to Operon, CKE archives, or Brainly Plus. Feature 04 closes that gap with a full-length timed mock test, per-topic diagnostic, and a 6-week revision plan.

The multi-year LTV argument

An E8-acquired family isn't a one-shot. The child enters liceum in September and is on the matura track for four years. An E8-acquired family is worth ~4-6× a klasa-5-acquired family on blended LTV. The funnel is a strategic acquisition pillar with a multi-year tail, not a feature.
